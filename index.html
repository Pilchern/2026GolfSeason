Standards of Play

Season Rules

Near-official PGA-style rules straight out of the USGA Rules of Golf. Real scores logged in 18Birdies, no cutting corners — full and fair competition every round.

Scoring
  • Gross score determines event winner unless otherwise noted
  • Net score side bets are optional and event-specific
  • Scorecards signed and attested by both players after each round
  • Disputes resolved on the spot using the USGA Rules of Golf
On-Course Standards
  • Putt everything out in stroke play — no gimmies
  • Play the ball down (lift, clean, and place only if course mandates)
  • No soft concessions in stroke play
  • Loose impediments may be moved; ball must not move
  • If your ball moves at address, it's a one-stroke penalty and replace
Out of Bounds & Lost Ball
  • OB or lost ball: stroke and distance — replay from previous spot, +1 penalty
  • Example: tee shot OB → hitting 3 from the tee
  • 3-minute search limit before a ball is officially lost
  • White stakes mark OB; ball on the line is still in
When Stakes Are Missing
  • Walk the course before play. If white/yellow/red stakes are missing or inconsistent, agree on rulings on the first tee — written in 18Birdies notes
  • No white stakes (OB): default to property lines, paved roads, fences, parking lots, and "out-of-play" signage as OB
  • No yellow stakes (penalty area): any obvious water hazard plays as a yellow penalty area — stroke-and-distance or back-on-the-line drop (+1)
  • No red stakes (lateral): any water bordering the hole laterally plays red — two-club-length drop where it last crossed (+1)
  • If markings disagree, the more restrictive interpretation wins (e.g., questionable OB plays as OB)
  • Any pre-round ruling stands for both players for the entire round — no mid-round renegotiation
  • Disputes in the moment: stop, talk it through, and agree before the next stroke — when in doubt, the more restrictive ruling holds
Provisional Ball
  • Announce "provisional" before hitting — required, or the new ball becomes the ball in play
  • Play the provisional if original may be OB or lost outside a penalty area
  • If original is found in bounds within 3 minutes, abandon the provisional
  • If not found, the provisional becomes the ball in play with stroke-and-distance penalty
Penalty Areas (Water)
  • Yellow stakes: stroke-and-distance, OR drop on line of entry going back as far as you like (+1)
  • Red stakes (lateral): same as yellow, plus a two-club-length drop from where it last crossed (+1)
  • You may play the ball as it lies inside a penalty area with no penalty
  • No grounding the club in the penalty area before the stroke
Drops & Relief
  • Drop from knee height, within one club length, no closer to the hole
  • Cart path / immovable obstruction: free relief, nearest point of complete relief + one club length, no closer to hole
  • Unplayable lie: +1 penalty — drop within two club lengths, on the line back, or replay from previous spot
  • Ground under repair / casual water: free relief, nearest point of complete relief + one club length
  • Embedded ball in the general area: free relief, drop within one club length, no closer to the hole
Bunkers
  • No grounding the club in the sand before the stroke
  • Loose impediments may be removed (no penalty if ball moves while doing so — replace it)
  • Unplayable in a bunker: drop in the bunker (+1) or back-on-the-line outside the bunker (+2)
  • Rake the bunker after every shot
Match Play Format
  • Holes are won, lost, or halved — lowest score on the hole wins it
  • Concessions allowed: opponents may concede a putt, hole, or the match
  • Match ends when one player is up more holes than remain (e.g., 4&3)
  • If all square through 18, play sudden-death extra holes until decided
  • Wrong order of play: opponent may require the stroke to be replayed
Stroke Play Format
  • Lowest 18-hole gross score wins the event
  • Every stroke counts — putt everything out, no concessions
  • One-shot penalty for any rule infraction unless otherwise noted
  • Ties broken by sudden-death playoff starting at hole 1
Format Schedule
  • Stroke play: Opening, First Major, Second Major, Final Major
  • Match play: Bittersweet Redemption, Signature Round 2
  • First & Final Major are always pure stroke play
  • Even mix of stroke and match across the rest of the slate
Major Championships
  • Three majors carry the heaviest point values (250, 300, 400)
  • Strictest conditions: no gimmies, no concessions, ball played down
  • All three majors are 18-hole stroke play
  • Final Major at 400 points can swing the entire season
Signature Events
  • Bittersweet: No quitting under any circumstances
  • Quitting at Bittersweet = automatic event loss + optional 3-hole deduction
  • Both signature events played as 18-hole match play
  • Concessions allowed but match must be played to a decision
Pace & Etiquette
  • Ready golf encouraged; honors taken on the tee in stroke play
  • Repair pitch marks, replace divots, rake bunkers
  • No advice between competitors except in foursomes/team formats
  • Phones silent on the course; rangefinders allowed (no slope in competition)
Weather & Rainouts
  • Round counts as official if at least 13 holes are completed by both players
  • If 12 holes or fewer: round is voided and the event must be replayed
  • If a major (≥250 pts) is voided, the makeup must happen within 21 days; if not playable, points roll over to the next major (added to that event's pot)
  • If a non-major is voided and can't be rescheduled, the event awards 0 points to both players
  • Lightning: clear the course immediately, no exceptions — partial rounds resume from the same hole if course reopens within 90 minutes
  • Replay scheduling: trailing player in season standings picks the makeup date from agreed-upon options
  • Casual water and lift/clean/place are in effect under wet conditions when the course mandates
Season Tiebreakers
  • If point totals are tied at season end, tiebreakers apply in this order:
  • 1. Winner of the Final Major takes The Cup outright
  • 2. If Final Major was tied/split: most majors won across the season
  • 3. Best head-to-head record (event wins minus event losses)
  • 4. Lowest total gross strokes across all completed events
  • 5. Sudden-death playoff at Cog Hill #4, hole 1 onward, soonest available date both players can make
  • Single-event ties (e.g., tied gross score in stroke play): split the points evenly between both players
